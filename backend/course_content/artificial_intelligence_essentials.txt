ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE ESSENTIALS

Artificial intelligence is the broader field of creating systems that can perform
tasks that usually require human intelligence, such as reasoning, language use,
planning, perception, and decision making.

Relationship to machine learning:
- AI is the broad umbrella.
- Machine learning is a major subfield of AI.
- Deep learning is a specialized area within machine learning.

Classical AI:
Classical AI focuses on logic, search, planning, and explicit rules.
Examples include path finding, game trees, expert systems, and symbolic reasoning.

Search and planning:
AI agents often solve problems by exploring possible actions and selecting the best
path toward a goal. Algorithms such as breadth-first search, depth-first search,
and A* are common examples.

Knowledge representation:
An AI system needs a way to represent facts, relationships, and uncertainty.
Knowledge graphs, rule systems, and probability models are all examples.

Machine learning driven AI:
Modern AI often learns from examples instead of depending only on rules.
This includes image recognition, speech recognition, recommendation systems, and
language models.

Neural networks:
Neural networks are layered mathematical models inspired loosely by the brain.
They transform input data through multiple layers to learn complex patterns.

Generative AI:
Generative AI creates new content such as text, images, code, audio, or video.
Large language models predict likely next tokens and are trained on massive text
collections. Diffusion models learn to generate images by reversing a noising
process.

Responsible AI:
AI systems must be designed with safety, privacy, fairness, and reliability in
mind. Good AI practice includes:
- evaluating bias,
- protecting user data,
- explaining limitations,
- monitoring misuse,
- keeping a human review path when stakes are high.

Prompting and grounding:
Language models are more reliable when given clear instructions and grounded
context. Retrieval-augmented generation improves factuality by feeding relevant
source material to the model before it answers.

Good mental model:
Think of AI systems as decision tools with strengths and limits. They can be fast
and creative, but they can also be confidently wrong if their inputs, data, or
guardrails are weak.
