Chapter 1 Artificial Intelligence and Agents

The history of AI is a history of fantasies, possibilities,
demonstrations, and promise. Ever since Homer wrote of mechanical
“tripods” waiting on the gods at dinner, imagined mechanical
assistants have been a part of our culture. However, only in the last
half century have we, the AI community, been able to build
experimental machines that test hypotheses about the mechanisms of
thought and intelligent behavior and thereby demonstrate mechanisms
that formerly existed only as theoretical possibilities.

– Bruce Buchanan [ 2005 ]

This book is about artificial intelligence (AI), a field built on centuries
of thought, which has been a recognized discipline for over 60
years. As well as solving
practical tasks, AI provides
tools to test hypotheses about the nature of thought itself.
Deep scientific and engineering problems have already been solved and
many more are waiting to be solved. Many practical applications are
currently deployed and the potential exists for an almost unlimited
number of future applications. This book presents the principles
that underlie intelligent computational agents.
