Part I Agents in the World

What are Agents and How Can They be Built?

Part II Reasoning and Planning
with Certainty

How can an agent represent its knowledge, reason, and plan,
under the assumption that it knows both what is true in the world and
the effects of its actions?

Part III Learning and Reasoning with Uncertainty

How can an agent learn and reason,
relaxing the assumption that it knows what is in the world?

Part IV Planning and Acting with Uncertainty

How can an agent plan and act,
relaxing the assumption that it knows the effects of its actions
and the current state of the world?

Part V Representing Individuals
and Relations

How can an agent reason, learn, and act in more complex environments
where there are individuals (things, entities, objects) with
complex relations among them?

Part VI The Big Picture

What are the social impacts of AI, and what are the likely future
scenarios for AI science and technology?
